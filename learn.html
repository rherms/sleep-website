Learn

Information about sleep debt and its effects on the body.

Quick facts

  • The average adult needs anywhere from 7-9 hours of sleep every night
  • Sleeping less than this needed amount will result in an accumulation of sleep debt
  • To eliminate your sleep debt, you need to sleep more than your needed amount, hour for hour.
  • Because of sleep debt, you may get 8 or even 9 hours of sleep, and still feel tired.

Example

Let's assume a college student, who needs 8 hours a night, sleeps according to the hours above. If the student needs 8 hours a night, their total sleep need is 56 hours a week. However, in this hypothetical week, the student got 48 hours. This means that the sleep debt accumulated this week was a total of 8 hours, or a whole day's worth of sleep.

Your Sleep Debt

Sleep debt has been calculated before. Researchers placed individuals in an isolated environment and witnessed people sleep for 14 hours for multiple days. Slowly, the individuals would get fewer hours of sleep as they paid off their debt. When it was completely eliminated, they slept for 8 hours in isolated conditions. Therefore sleep debt is possible to calculate, but it is impractical to calculate. You'd have to be isolated for weeks in order to see just how much debt you've accumulated over your life.

There's an inverse relationship between sleep debt and sleep latency. That is, the faster you fall asleep, the higher your sleep debt, and the longer it takes for you to fall asleep, the lower your sleep debt must be.
